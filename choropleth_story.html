Interactive Choropleth - How it Happened

I originally wrote this mapping library to track live product signups, adding the ability to subset by multiple metrics in order to guide our marketing teams. In that case, the TSV from which the visualization drew its bars and colors was updated every minute by a cron job. I have since reused the code to better understand the world we inhabit.

I crafted a map of income and population changes in the US in order to understand something that fascinates me - the geo-econometrics of our nation's recovery. I recently purchased An Illustrated Guide to Income in the United States and was underwhelmed. I realized that my reaction was less due to a failure of the work, but of the medium. When you look at the map to the right, and the other maps in the trailer, there's an instinctive desire to understand how the underlying data relate to eachother.

The reality of data visualization is that some metrics lend themselves inherently to static or interactive visualization - trying to intuit the growth of an economy definitely falls in the latter category:
